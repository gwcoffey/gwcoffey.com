THINGS HOSPITALS AND AIRPORTS HAVE IN COMMON

Geoff Coffey
/ 13 February 2016

  • Ugly floors

  • Ugly furniture

  • Strange smells

  • Long waits

  • People trying to cry discreetly without access to privacy

  • People who haven't seen each other in a long time

  • A man occasionally touches my privates

  • X-ray machines

  • Questionable food

  • Paid parking

  • Sometimes I don't eat for some time before I go there

    I have issues...

  • Newspapers still exist

  • Free hand sanitizer

  • Shops with an eclectic assortment of merchandise

  • People in uniforms delivering bad news

  • Extensive regulation governing their operation

  • This list is longer than I thought it would be

    I really only made it because I am at the airport and a sorrowful young woman is trying to be discreet about her crying three feet from me. It is breaking my heart. I texted my wife and both my children so now I'm connecting with you.
