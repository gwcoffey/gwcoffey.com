WHAT I LOVE ABOUT MUSIC

Geoff Coffey
/ 8 April 2016

  • I'm definitely not as in to music as many of you.

  • I own a decent pile of albums (iTunes says I have about 400) and I listen to music every day while I work.

  • But despite my father's best efforts I've never been blown away or deeply moved by the music itself.

  • He loves classical, especially piano, and describes some of his listening experiences in almost religious terms. He is genuinely surprised that I'm not able to feel the same way.

  • It is "pretty" I guess but I would never just sit and listen to music and not do something else.

  • And I very rarely see live music because I find it boring.

  • That said there is some music that I would say is important to me. Favorites that I've loved for a long time and that have moved me deeply. Many of my favorites were on my playlist when I was a teenager, and still are. And newer stuff that's resonating today.

  • And as I've gotten older I've sort of figured out that what matters to me about music is:

  • Sincerity

  • Story

  • And surprise.

  • My favorite musicians are the likes of:

  • Tom Waits

  • John Prine

  • Emmy Lou Harris

  • But also more esoteric lyricists like:

  • Michael Stipe

  • Regina Spektor

  • Elvis Costello

  • And I also like strong female voices like:

  • Neko Case

  • Gin Wigmore

  • Luscious Jackson

  • I suppose what I get from it is different than some people but my goodness, when John Prine sings...

  • ..."A person can't tell his best friend he loves him. Til time has stopped breathing you're alone on the hill."...

    http://youtu.be/QMG7ud_t7o8

  • I feel a little jealous that I don't understand music they way others do, but then for just a second I imagine I really do.
