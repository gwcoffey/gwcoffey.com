WAYS I TELL MY DAUGHTER TO STOP TALKING SO MUCH

Geoff Coffey
/ 10 August 2015

Girl can talk.

  • There are too many words coming out of the hole in your face.

    Her favorite. She asked me to tell her teachers to use this one but I did not.

  • Sophia why don't we play a game. I talk to mom and you don't.

  • [when telemarketers call] Sophia, it's for you!

  • I think the cat wants you to talk to her now.

  • You need to take a breather and so do my ears.

  • Siri is currently available for conversations.

  • I think we've circled back to the beginning of this story now so that means you're done.

  • [she wants to be an actor] Why don't you practice acting by pretending to be mute? Those are the sorts of parts that win oscars.

  • Someday you're going to grow up and move away and then it will be so quiet here I'll cry. Just kidding it'll be awesome.

  • Pretend I'm saying whatever your teachers say to get you to stop talking.

  • I'm going to bed now. Feel free to keep talking to the cupboards.

  • Sophia, grandma's old. She doesn't have enough time left to listen to this whole story.
