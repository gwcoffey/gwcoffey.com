Mythical creatures I would like to be

Geoff Coffey
/ 18 July 2016

  • Everybody knows the four elements are:

  • Earth

  • Wind

  • Fire

  • Water

  • (Some ivory tower academics have proposed a ludicrously complex system with over a hundred supposed elements, an idea so absurd I see no reason to refute it.)

  • These elements, in mixtures of various amounts, make up all around us.

  • He imbued the elements with consciousness and in that way made life.

  • From wind He made angels.

  • From fire the most vile devils.

  • From Earth the beasts of land and sea.

  • And what of Man?

  • A plain inspection will reveal that Man is made of the same stuff as the beast.

  • But he also has within him the virtue of the angel.

  • And the vice of the devil.

  • So we can see Man is the union of the elements, beast, angel, and devil. In harmony or in conflict. Diverse and unique.

  • But there is an obvious gap in this otherwise perfect plan.

  • What of water?

  • Surely God in all His exactitude did not construct a system nearly — but not quite — perfect.

  • It stands to reason that He made of water some creature heretofore unknown.

  • What is this being?

  • And what are its attributes?

  • And surely whatever it is, it too formed a part of the complete circle of Man.

  • One quarter of our nature, secreted away. To be revealed? And if so, when?

  • I would be whatever God made from water.

  • But only for a while.

  • So I could understand myself more fully.
