JUST THE GOOD PARTS

Geoff Coffey
/ 18 October 2015

Examples of good things stripped of the inessential to reveal their truest value.

  • Caramel

    The overt richness of butter and sugar freed from the conformist structures of flour-based baking.

  • Halloween

    They unbridled joy of holiday celebration unburdened by moral imperative.

  • Raclette

    Melted cheese by itself, which is all we really want, elevated from gaucherie by a French sounding name. Ooh la la so sophisticated!

  • The American Flag

    Patriotic assertion of national pride unconflicted by … well … almost everything right now.

  • The List App (double whammy)

    Public creative outlet unmoderated AND social interaction unsullied by actual social interaction.

  • Netflix

    Buffy the Vampire Slayer unhaunted by commercials and midseason breaks.

  • Smoked Brisket from Little Miss Barbecue

    Seasoned meat and smoke unadulterated by spices, sauces, or complex sides.
