THINGS I DO THAT CAUSE BOOKISH PEOPLE TO DOUBT MY COMMITMENT TO THE CAUSE

Geoff Coffey
/ 17 February 2016

  • Send all my paper books to goodwill and switch to almost 100% eBooks.

    I like reading eBooks so much better unless pictures or layout are important to the book.

  • Forget the title of books and the names of authors all the time.

  • Have to ask what "TBR" means when I attend a bookish conference thing in New York with @ChrisK.

  • Related: Avoid keeping a TBR list.

  • Feel completely overwhelmed by shelves full of books.

    Or any other product for that matter. Bookstores, libraries, the spice aisle, blockbuster (lol remember them?), and a long list of names all stress me out.

  • Read very slowly.

    Poor me.

  • Not want to talk about a book for a few days after I've read it until I've had time to process my thoughts.

  • Not care a whit about spoilers.

    Unless the mystery is the whole point, it doesn't bother me at all to read, hear, or be told how a book ends.

  • Read on my phone.

    It's not my favorite way to read a book but I do it all the time because it's there when I have a few minutes of down time.
