Vonnegut, Ranked

Geoff Coffey
/ 15 December 2016

Sentimentally skewed by the order/age in which I read them. Featuring the covers I owned. Limiting myself to major works I read cover to cover here.

  1. My first, around age 15. Probably higher ranking than it deserves because it blew my little teenage mind.

  2. My third, also when I was 15. Probably my actual favorite.

  3. Not until I was 17 or 18 but up-ranked by awesomeness.

  4. My fifth, when I was 16. Also blew my mind.

  5. The fourth I read, also when I was 15, maybe 16. A mixed bag but this collection has some real gems.

  6. Read when I was 17. Upranked because I saw Vonnegut speak in Indianapolis when this was published and he signed my copy.

  7. The second I read, when I was 15. I spent a lot of time thinking about this book after reading it.

  8. Read this one (an Deadeye Dick) on a long summer trip when I was 16.

  9. I did a school paper on this my sophomore year. I remember it was delightfully weird and cynical.

  10. When I was 16. I didn't love this on my first reading but I've since grown to love it more.

  11. When I was 17. One of the last for me and for some reason I've never re-read it. Probably time to. I remember loving it.

  12. I remember reading this surreptitiously in class and not wanting to stop. Don't remember much else.

  13. When I was 16 or 17. I haven't read it since. Again I probably should.

  14. When I was 18 just to round out my obsession. Also need to re-read.

  15. This wasn't published until four years after I finished high school. Not much of a novel. More the ramblings of a cranky brilliant mind too tired to pound its thoughts into something cohesive. Still essential Vonnegut.

  16. I can't find the cover I had. It was black, but not this one. Read when I was 16 and I didn't really like it much.
