Lesser of Two Evils

Geoff Coffey
/ 13 October 2016

  • Look.

  • There's a lot of talk about the Trump/Clinton equivalency.

  • "Trump may be bad, but Clinton is a liar, a crook, and maybe even a murderer."

  • If I push back against these claims I get drowned in a torrent of half-truths, misinformation, and links to right-wing blogs with shady stories of unknown provenance.

  • Some of this is "true" in the sense that Clinton has made some very stupid mistakes (E.g. emails).

  • Some of this is deeply misleading in that the facts are sad and harrowing but Clinton bears no actual guilt (E.g. Benghazi.)

  • Some of this is nonsense. (E.g. Defending rapists in court.)

  • And some of it is unnuanced and hateful (E.g supporting her husband in a deeply personal and difficult situation.)

  • You say to me, "You have to dig deep, search for it to find the dirty truth, ignore the MSM and their lies."

  • I myself have called Clinton "unlikable" or "unpresidential" in the past.

    I've come to terms with the fact that this is at least 99% misogyny and I'm sorry for it.

  • And I myself have called her untrustworthy, opportunistic, etc...

    I honestly have a very hard time judging my own feelings here. I'm concerned that this too is at least 50% misogyny. I grew up in an era where Clinton was attacked constantly in the media as the butch/ugly/undignified/overbearing/etc. wife of the president and I'm afraid it poisoned the well of my own mind.

  • So I'm honestly not sure how "bad" she is. My rational sense is that she's not particularly bad at all.

    She's an opportunistic politician which I would shrug off in a man and it comes across as amplified because she's a woman and because of her history of unrelenting partisan assault.

  • But maybe she's particularly dishonest.

  • Maybe she's particularly opportunistic.

  • Aside: She's certainly *deeply qualified*. Let's not forget that.

  • But here's the thing.

  • You don't have to dig deep, consult shady sources, lean on cultural bias, and trust dysphemistic partisan summaries of thousand page reports to spot Trump's evil.

  • You only have to listen to him speak.

  • And watch him move.

  • You don't need to decipher hidden clues to know he...

  • ...Mocks a physically disabled reporter like the worst of the worst eighth grade bully.

  • ...Denigrates a distinguished American war hero who was tortured repeatedly and spent five years as a prisoner of war.

  • ...Proposes an outright ban on immigrants based on their faith and/or ethnicity.

  • ...Disparages millions of Mexican immigrants based only on their nation of origin.

  • ...Argues for the deliberate murder of innocent family members of our military enemies.

  • ...Verbally attacks numerous American citizens openly and publicly because they disagree with his politics.

  • ...Insists an American Judge is unfit to serve because of his parents' ethnic heritage.

  • ...Brags about the size of his penis during a televised debate.

  • ...Disparages the physical appearance of his political opponent's wife.

  • ...Boasts about his ability to sexually assault women at will.

  • ...Promotes a view of America rooted in fear, hatred, and anger.

  • ...Lies openly and brazenly about easily verifiable facts.

  • ...Resorts to base xenophobia to explain away crime, climate change, and every other difficult problem.

  • You don't have to like Clinton. But to suggest that she "just as bad" as Trump is frankly ridiculous.

  • Donald Trump is not fit to share my dinner table, let alone be our president.

    And I say this as a person who spoke strongly against Bill Clinton's impropriety when he was president. And who was not a Bush supporter but spoke out routinely against the over the top anti-Bush rhetoric coming from the left.

  • Trump is different.

  • He is awful.

  • All I'm trying to say is...

  • If you think Clinton and Trump are equivalent—both evil—you're wrong.

  • Clinton is somewhere between typical-politician and more-than-average-opportunist depending on how willing you are to trust highly partisan interpretations of her political enemies.

    (Or she's evil if you believe utter bull.)

  • Trump is clearly, by his own words, right in front of your face, indisputably horrible.

  • These are not the same thing.

  • Vote for Clinton.

  • Vote for Johnson.

  • Vote for McMullin.

  • Vote for Stein.

  • Or write in someone else.

  • But for the love of all things we hold dear as a nation and a species...

  • Please don't vote for Trump.
