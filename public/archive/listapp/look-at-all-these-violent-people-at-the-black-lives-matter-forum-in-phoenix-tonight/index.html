Look At All These Violent People At The Black Lives Matter Forum In Phoenix Tonight

Geoff Coffey
/ 19 July 2016

I saw on Facebook that Black Lives Matter is a violent terrorist organization so we went to a meeting to see how bad it was. Look what we found.

  • Phoenix Mayor Greg Stanton

    He wants anarchy.

  • This panel speaker talking about educational access.

    She looks meek but she wants to destroy America.

  • This lawyer answering questions about the ills of for-profit prisons and inequity in the justice system.

    Thug.

  • This crusader for Native American rights who introduced herself first in Navajo then in English.

    Hide your children.

  • The guy in the back who said he wants to bring people peace through God although, he said, you don't have to be religious to find that peace. And that he wants to teach children skills to break the cycle of violence.

    Yikes.

  • This college student who wants to run for public office when she meets the age requirement.

    I felt threatened.

  • This pastor of a community church who sits on the city council committee for community relations.

    When he said his wife was ill and he was missing her I knew he was a danger to society.

  • The assistant chief of police.

    Pretended to be nice by answering questions openly and honestly and by listening. Basically a monster.

  • This woman in a head scarf.

    Need I say more?

  • This state legislator.

    A convenient cover for his revolutionary proclivities.

  • This other state legislator.

    She probably has an assault weapon under her chair.

  • This recent ASU graduate giving an inspiring speech about the importance of community involvement.

    Trigger warning: she was super positive and encouraging.

  • This Large Black Man encouraging everyone to find a way to get involved and soliciting suggestions and commitment.

    He was inspiring and impassioned. People like that can do a lot of damage.

  • [not pictured] The grieving mother calling on all members of the community to break the code of silence and demand justice for victims of black on black crime.

    [I can't bring myself to say anything sarcastic about this poor woman or her moving words]

  • [not pictured] The uniformed police officers at the door warning everyone that it rained and was slippery so to please watch your step.

    Some people just want to see the world burn.
