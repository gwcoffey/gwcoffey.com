CARDINAL SINS OF NEWS SITE DESIGN

Geoff Coffey
/ 16 January 2016

This is why reading on the web sucks.

  • Not sizing pictures so the stuff I'm reading moves around as parts continue to load.

  • Splitting an article into several pages for no reason at all except boosting page views.

  • Ads that make noise.

  • Ads that take over the whole screen and load slowly enough that I accidentally click on them.

  • Ads that open a new page or, even worse, another app.

  • Things that disappear when I start scrolling and reappear when I stop.

  • Overriding standard iPhone scroll behavior and trying to mimic it yourself with touch-tracking. You will fail.

  • Pictures and sidebars that overlap the text if I zoom in.

  • Ads that open on touch instead of tap so I trigger them when I'm trying to scroll.

  • Anything that scrolls inside a page that scrolls.

  • Ads that look like links to more articles except that they're articles I would never actually want to read.

  • Impossible-to-not-look-at pictures associated with those article-ads. Like boobs in tight t-shirts and guys with weird muscles.

  • Headlines that way over-promise.
