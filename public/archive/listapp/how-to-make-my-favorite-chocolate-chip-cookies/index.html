How to Make my Favorite Chocolate Chip Cookies

Geoff Coffey
/ 15 May 2016

Making oodles of these for @imc’s graduation open house right now.

  • 375 F oven.

  • Put 20 tablespoons of butter in a pan and melt it.

  • And then brown it.

  • Off heat stir in another stick of cold butter until it melts.

  • Put the butter, 7 ounces of granulated sugar, 10.5 ounces of brown sugar, 2 teaspoons of salt and 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract in a bowl.

  • Whisk.

  • Add two eggs and two egg yolks.

  • Whisk.

  • Let it sit for a few minutes then whisk again. Then rest again and whisk again. Repeat until it becomes pretty smooth. Usually takes three rounds or so.

  • Whisk together 17.5 ounces of all purpose flour plus a teaspoon of baking powder.

  • Add the flour to the liquid a bit at a time...

  • ...stirring to mostly incorporate after each addition...

  • ...until it is all in there.

  • Stir in 2.5 cups of semi-sweet chocolate chips.

  • Roll in balls and space them evenly on a baking sheet. I do 2 tablespoon portions.

  • Bake on the middle rack for 10 minutes. Do not over brown. They should be browning slightly around the edges. Then let them cool on the sheet for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack or towel.
