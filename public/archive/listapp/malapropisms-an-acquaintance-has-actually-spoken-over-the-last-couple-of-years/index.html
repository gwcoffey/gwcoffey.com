MALAPROPISMS AN ACQUAINTANCE HAS ACTUALLY SPOKEN OVER THE LAST COUPLE OF YEARS

Geoff Coffey
/ 1 August 2015

I used to think Shakespeare’s malapropism-spewing characters were over the top. Then I met this guy…

  • "It was an adjunct failure."

    He meant abject.

  • "We need to support and abstain one another."

    He meant sustain.

  • "It conosotes something different."

    He meant connotes.

  • "Him and all his little dominions doing his bidding."

    He meant minions.

  • "I went to conjole her during this difficult time."

    He meant console.

  • "There is a little asterfix next to it."

    He meant asterisk.

  • "Can you confer that?"

    He meant confirm.

  • "I suspect he has outerior motives."

    He meant ulterior.

  • "The fence encumbered the whole thing."

    He meant encompassed.
