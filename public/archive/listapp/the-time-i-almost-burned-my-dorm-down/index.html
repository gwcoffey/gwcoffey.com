THE TIME I ALMOST BURNED MY DORM DOWN

Geoff Coffey
/ 13 January 2016

I exaggerated. I didn’t almost burn my dorm down. But I did cause a scene.

  • When I was a freshman at Purdue University living on the eight floor of Shreve Hall in 1993,

  • My friend received a package that was packed with those biodegradable packing peanuts.

  • The kind that dissolve instantly in water.

  • I think they're made of corn.

  • They were cool. I decided to see if they would burn. Because they kind of smelled like popcorn.

  • I was by the window where my friend had his ashtray and lighter on the sill.

  • So I lit one.

  • It burned more aggressively than I expected.

  • Like I thought it would be more like styrofoam and sort of shrivel and melt.

  • But it just caught fire like paper.

  • I didn't want to burn my fingers so I dropped it in the ashtray.

  • But it was so airy and it was aflame. It didn't want to sit still. It was like a very tiny very ugly very corny version of those floating paper lanterns.

  • I didn't worry much when it fluttered out the window.

  • Surely it would burn itself out mid flight.

  • And it was such a tiny thing. I had been starting fires in the fireplace all my life and it's HARD. This thing couldn't do any damage.

  • We watched it fall. It continued to flame.

  • It drifted so slowly but it continued to flame.

  • It was the end of a hot summer. There was a long hedge of bushes all along the side of the building.

  • It slipped behind the bushes.

  • And within seconds the flames spread across the bushes. Eight, ten, twelve feet of tall dry bushes in full flame.
