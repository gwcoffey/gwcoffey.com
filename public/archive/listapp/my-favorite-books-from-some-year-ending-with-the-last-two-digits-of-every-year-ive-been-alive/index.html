MY FAVORITE BOOKS FROM SOME YEAR ENDING WITH THE LAST TWO DIGITS OF EVERY YEAR I'VE BEEN ALIVE

Geoff Coffey
/ 29 March 2016

Inspired by @amieshmamie but I don’t read contemporary books enough so let’s see how far we can stretch this concept.

  • 1775: The Sorrows of Young Werther by Goethe

  • 1976: Roots: The Saga of an American Family by Alex Haley

  • 1977: A Scanner Darkly by Philip K Dick

  • 1078: The Proslogion by Anselm

  • 1979: Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy by Douglas Adams

  • 1980: A Confederacy of Dunces by John Kennedy Toole

  • 1881: Portrait of a Lady by Henry James

  • 1982: The BFG by Roald Dahl

  • 1883: Treasure Island by Robert Louis Stevenson

  • 1984: The Dictionary of Troublesome Words by Bill Bryson

  • 1985: Blood Meridian by Cormac McCarthy

  • 1986: Speaker for the Dead by Orson Scott Card

  • 1587: Holinshed's Chronicles (2nd Edition) by Raphael Holinshed et al

    (I haven't actually read this but it was hugely influential to Shakespeare, the source for most of his histories and the inspiration for several other plays.)

  • 1988: The Mezzanine by Nicholson Baker

  • 1989: A Prayer for Owen Meany by John Irving

  • 1990: Hocus Pocus by Kurt Vonnegut

  • 1991: The Gold Bug Variations by Richard Powers

  • 1992: All the Pretty Horses by Cormac McCarthy

  • 1993: The Giver by Lois Lowry

  • 1794: The Age of Reason by Thomas Paine

  • 1995: Wicked: The Life and Times of the Wicked Witch of the West by Gregory Maguire

  • 1996: Angela's Ashes by Frank McCourt

  • 1597: Richard II by William Shakespeare

  • 1998: Shakespeare: The Invention of the Human by Harold Bloom

  • 1999: Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban by JK Rowling

  • 300 BCE: Elements by Euclid

  • 2001: The Life of Pi by Yann Martel

  • 2002: Bad Astronomy by Phil Plait (@BadAstronomer)

  • 1603: Hamlet by William Shakespeare

  • 1904: The Tale of Benjamin Bunny by Beatrix Potter

  • 2005: The Princess Academy by Shannon Hale

  • 2006: The Road by Cormac McCarthy

  • 1607: Antony and Cleopatra by William Shakespeare

  • 1908: Dorothy and the Wizard of Oz by L Frank Baum

  • 2009: FileMaker Pro: The Missing Manual (Third Edition) by Geoff Coffey and Susan Prosser

    (Definitely the most exciting book released this year.)

  • 2010: How to Live Safely in a Science Fictional Universe by Charles Yu

  • 2011: The Swerve: How the World Became Modern by Stephen Greenblatt

  • 2012: Lone Survivors: How We Came to be the Only Humans on Earth by Chris Stringer

  • 2013: Americanah by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

  • 2014: Krabat and the Sorcerer's Mill by Otfried Preussler (Republished)

    Fun fact: Cover art by @ChrisK.

  • 1215: The Magna Carta by King John of England et al

  • 1916: A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man by James Joyce
